Mechanical Team

Formula Snail

October - November 2025

A roller-coaster-style toy inspired by Formula racing and the film Turbo, with mechanical design inspiration from retractable tape measures and Hot Wheels toys.

AutoCAD SOLIDWORKS 3D-Printing Sketching MS Excel/Word

Objective

  • Design and prototype a children’s toy that transforms stored mechanical energy into motion through a non-motorized propulsion system
  • Apply iterative prototyping to produce and evaluate design iterations within a short timeline, optimizing for mechanical feasibility and user experience
  • Develop a modular track system that can be customizable to enhance playability and encourage creativity
  • Ensure the toy is intuitive, engaging, and safe for children ages 6 and older

Process

  • Contributed to early-stage team ideation, evaluating multiple toy concepts to select a mechanically feasible design within time and resource constraints
  • Led track system development, designing modular straight and curved geometries with universal connectors to enable customizable layouts and repeatable play
  • Refined connector geometry after evaluating four concept mechanisms, selecting the structurally secure and intuitive locking method
  • Coordinated weekly project milestones in Excel to align design, prototyping, and testing efforts across the team
  • Modeled and refined track profiles in SOLIDWORKS to optimize guidance, lateral stability, and manufacturability
  • Prototyped straight and curved segments using cardboard, laser-cut wood, and 3D-printed PLA to compare rigidity, friction, and precision
  • Tested kerf-cut wood for curved track segments; after observing innacurate bend geometry, transitioned to a 3D-printed base to improve geometric precision and reduce friction

Results

  • Successfully developed a fully functional, torsion-spring–powered snail that travels smoothly along a modular track system
  • Achieved consistent propulsion at a controlled, child-safe speed through track geometry optimization and friction reduction
  • Developed a modular track system that can be configured in a variety of ways via universal connectors and purposefully engineered track pieces
  • Demonstrated final performance through live testing (see video)
